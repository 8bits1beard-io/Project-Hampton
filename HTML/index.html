About Project: Hampton

Why I Built This

Hi, I'm Joshua Walderbach, and I created Project: Hampton because I believe everyone should have access to the incredible power of AI-assisted coding. In an era where technology is evolving at lightning speed, the ability to build software with AI isn't just a skill ‚Äì it's a superpower that should be democratized.

My goal with this project is simple: to create the most comprehensive, accessible, and engaging platform for learning how to code with AI assistants. I want to take you from zero to building production-ready applications in just 30 days, using the same tools and techniques that professional developers are adopting today.

In Honor of Tom Hampton

5th Grade Teacher, Cleveland Elementary, Cedar Rapids, IA

This project is lovingly named after Mr. Tom Hampton, my 5th grade teacher at Cleveland Elementary School in Cedar Rapids, Iowa. He holds a special place in my heart as not only the first positive male role model I ever had, but also as the teacher who truly saw me. Mr. Hampton was the first educator who understood that I needed to learn at my own pace‚Äîhe let me soar when I was ready to run, and gently encouraged me when I struggled to find my footing.

What made him extraordinary wasn't just his kindness and unwavering support, but the way he genuinely invested himself in every single student who walked through his classroom door. He had this remarkable gift for seeing the potential in each of us and nurturing it until we could see it in ourselves. He didn't just teach us lessons from textbooks; he taught us to believe we could become the best versions of ourselves.

The true measure of his impact became clear many years later when I unexpectedly ran into him at a local pub. Without missing a beat, he remembered me‚Äînot just my name, but who I was as a person. As we caught up over the beer he insisted on buying, I realized that the caring teacher who had changed my life in 5th grade was still the same warm, generous soul who remembered a student decades later.

This project carries his name because his influence carries forward in everything I do.
